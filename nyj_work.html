Born a crime summary

Born a Crime is a book by a South African-based author ‘Trevor Noah,
in this book he describes his life as a mixed-race child raised in apartheid.
His mother is portrayed as a skillful, strong-willed, and determined woman.
Chapter one explores some of his mother’s traits like any African woman whereas
chapter two goes ahead to tell us how the apartheid time affected
his childhood especially when he went to his grandmother's time.

Bribery
This is seen in the novel when Trevor’s mother is said to have given a bride
of a huge sum of money to the law enforcers so that she can be able to stay in Johnasberg.
In Uganda, this incident has been reported to happen especially when the law forces take money from speeding drivers.

Indisciplined drivers.
The book portrays the passenger drivers at the time as unruly and indisciplined
especially when they try to fight a driver accusing him of taking their customers.
This occurs in Uganda where some of the drivers do not want other drivers
using their route and taking the passengers from the taxi stop.

Bravery
Bravery is shown by Trevor’s mother when she decides to illegally live in
a city that discriminates against her and has laws aimed at keeping her out of sight.
This act of bravery is a window to the extent to which women can go.
Bravery continues to prevail in Ugandan women and mothers as they go through their daily
share of misfortune and face it head-on.
